# Product

## Register

product

## Users

BuyWise serves two task-focused groups. Beta shoppers use the Android client while comparing products, asking AI for recommendations, checking details, and recording post-purchase feedback. Internal operators use the web admin console to maintain catalog data, inspect readiness, and debug release or indexing status.

## Product Purpose

BuyWise helps shoppers make clearer purchase decisions by combining product data, comparison, multimodal input, and AI explanations. Success means users can move from need expression to a shortlist and rationale without losing trust in the data, while operators can keep the catalog and beta workflows healthy.

## Brand Personality

Practical, trustworthy, and decisive. The product should feel like a calm shopping workbench, not a marketing showcase or a novelty chatbot. Copy should name the concrete action and the concrete state.

## Anti-references

Avoid SaaS landing-page theater, decorative AI glow, purple-blue gradient dashboards, glassmorphism, oversized rounded cards, and ecommerce layouts that bury product evidence behind decoration. Avoid making the admin console look like a consumer storefront.

## Design Principles

1. Put the task first: every screen should make the next shopping or operations action obvious.
2. Evidence before ornament: product names, prices, stock, ratings, reasons, and health states should carry the hierarchy.
3. One vocabulary across surfaces: admin, Android cards, chips, states, and errors should use compatible color and component semantics.
4. Calm density: shoppers need approachable cards, operators need scannable tables; both should avoid wasted space.
5. Honest AI: recommendation UI must show rationale and uncertainty without pretending to be magic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve visible focus states, touch targets of at least 44px on mobile, stable layouts for missing product data, and reduced-motion-safe state changes.
